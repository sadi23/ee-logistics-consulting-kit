# Bio Library Templates

Pre-written bio templates for independent logistics consultants. Each template targets a specific audience and medium. Fill in the bracketed fields with your details.

---

## Template 1: LinkedIn Profile (About Section)

**Tone:** First-person, peer-to-peer, results-oriented.
**Length:** 200–300 words.
**Audience:** Potential clients, referral partners, and recruiters scanning your profile.

```
I help [target client type, e.g. mid-market 3PLs] [solve specific problem, e.g. cut warehouse picking errors below 0.3%].

I have spent [number] years in [your specific area, e.g. warehouse control systems and distribution centre operations]. Across [number] engagements in [number] countries, I have helped clients [specific outcome 1, e.g. reduce peak-season overtime by 40%] and [specific outcome 2, e.g. achieve real-time inventory visibility without replacing their existing WMS].

My approach is practical. I do not sell multi-year transformations. I work with [specific context, e.g. mid-market teams that have outgrown spreadsheets but cannot justify a tier-1 integrator]. We focus on the 20% of changes that deliver 80% of the value.

What I bring:
- [Skill 1, e.g. WCS architecture and integration with existing WMS]
- [Skill 2, e.g. Peak-season capacity planning]
- [Skill 3, e.g. Regulatory compliance for CSRD and DSCSA]

Before consulting I was [previous relevant role, e.g. Head of Warehouse Systems at a 500-person 3PL]. That operational experience shapes everything I do — I know what works when the IT team is small and the pressure is high.

I work with clients across [geography or sector]. If your [specific pain point] keeps you up at night, let us talk.

[Link to your website or consultation booking page]
```

## Template 2: Speaker / Conference Bio

**Tone:** Third-person, credential-forward, designed for event programmes.
**Length:** 80–120 words.
**Audience:** Conference organisers, event attendees reading the programme.

```
[Full Name] is an independent logistics consultant specialising in [specific area, e.g. warehouse control systems for mid-market 3PLs]. Over [number] years, [he/she/they] has led [number] implementations across [number] countries, helping clients achieve [specific result 1] and [specific result 2].

[Name]'s work focuses on [specific approach, e.g. practical, incremental WCS deployments that integrate with existing WMS rather than replacing them]. [He/She/They] is a regular contributor to [publication or podcast name] and speaks on [specific topics, e.g. warehouse automation for the mid-market, CSRD compliance, and peak-season planning].

[Name] previously served as [previous role] at [previous company], bringing hands-on operational experience to every engagement.

[Optional: Link to website / LinkedIn]
```

## Template 3: Proposal / Engagement Bio

**Tone:** First-person, credibility-focused, direct.
**Length:** 100–150 words.
**Audience:** Procurement and decision-makers evaluating your proposal.

```
I bring [number] years of hands-on experience in [specific domain, e.g. warehouse control systems for mid-market logistics operators]. I have delivered [number] similar engagements for [client type], with measurable outcomes including [specific result 1] and [specific result 2].

This engagement is a direct fit with my expertise. I have:
- [Relevant experience point 1]
- [Relevant experience point 2]
- [Relevant experience point 3]

I understand that [client company name] operates in [specific context]. I have worked with companies of similar size and complexity, and I know the constraints you face — [specific constraint 1], [specific constraint 2]. My recommendations will be practical, not theoretical.

I am available to start [start date]. The investment for this engagement is [fee or range], and I estimate [timeline] to deliver the agreed outcomes.

I look forward to the opportunity to work with your team.
```

## Bio Maintenance & Version Control

Your bios are living documents, not static text. They must evolve as your business does.

-   **Use a Single Source of Truth:** Keep a master document (this workbook is a good start) in one place (e.g., a pinned note, a file in your main business folder). This is the *only* place you edit your core data and bio templates.
-   **When to Update:** Immediately after you achieve a new, quantifiable result. Did you just help a client cut their exception rate by another 10%? Add it to the master doc *now*, not at the end of the quarter.
-   **The Ripple-Down Update Process:**
    1.  Update the core data in your master workbook first (e.g., change "six implementations" to "seven").
    2.  Update your LinkedIn "About" section and "Featured" section. This is your most public-facing profile.
    3.  Update your master proposal template.
    4.  You don't need to revise old speaker bios for events that have passed, but use the new version for all future events.
-   **Review quarterly.** At the start of each quarter, read all your public-facing bios. Do they still reflect the work you are doing and the clients you are targeting? If not, adjust. This prevents brand drift.
