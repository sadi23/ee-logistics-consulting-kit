# Content Strategy — Logistics & Supply Chain LinkedIn

A sustainable 3×/week posting strategy for supply chain and logistics professionals. This isn't about becoming a content creator — it's about being *seen as* the person who understands what's happening in the industry.

---

## Core Philosophy

**Don't write like a consultant. Write like an operator who can also explain things.**

Logistics decision-makers (heads of supply chain, procurement directors, freight buyers, 3PL VPs) scroll LinkedIn the same way everyone else does. They stop on content that:

1. **Names a real problem** they've lived this week
2. **Gives them one useful frame** for thinking about it
3. **Is clearly written by someone who's done the work**

You don't need to break news. You need to *process* what's happening through an operational lens.

---

## Content Pillars

Pick 3 of these 5. Rotating between them gives enough variety without spreading too thin.

### Pillar 1: Operations & Execution
Real stories about moving freight, running warehouses, managing carriers, clearing customs.

**Why it works:** Shows you've been in the trenches. Builds credibility no thought leadership article can match.

**Post ideas:**
- "The hardest part about cross-docking isn't the dock — it's the paperwork. Here's what I learned after 50 implementations."
- "We cut dwell time at Southampton by 22% with one change to the carrier scorecard."
- "What nobody tells you about implementing a TMS: the first 90 days are about data hygiene, not software."

### Pillar 2: Tech & Automation
Warehouse automation, TMS/WMS decisions, AI in logistics, control towers, visibility platforms.

**Why it works:** Technology adoption is the #1 topic in supply chain right now. Operators are drowning in sales pitches. They want someone who can separate signal from noise.

**Post ideas:**
- "I've evaluated 14 warehouse automation RFPs this year. Here's the question 90% of vendors can't answer."
- "You don't need an AI control tower. You need someone to reconcile your inventory data first."
- "The real ROI from supply chain visibility isn't tracking — it's exception management."

### Pillar 3: Policy, Trade & Regulation
Tariffs, customs changes, Incoterms, sanctions, border controls, trade agreements.

**Why it works:** Policy changes move fast and have direct operational impact. Being the person who *understands what changed and what to do about it* builds an audience fast.

**Post ideas:**
- "The new EU CBAM rules start phasing in next quarter. Here's what it means for anyone importing steel or aluminium."
- "UK customs after the border target operating model — three things that actually changed this month."
- "How the Red Sea diversions reshaped ocean freight procurement in 2024 (and what's stuck for 2025)."

### Pillar 4: Career & Culture
Career paths in logistics, hiring trends, how to break into supply chain, working conditions, industry diversity.

**Why it works:** Career content performs well on LinkedIn because everyone's thinking about their next move. It also positions you as someone invested in the industry's future.

**Post ideas:**
- "Supply chain isn't a 'sexy' career. But 10 years in, here's why I'd pick it again."
- "The logistics roles that will pay £100k+ by 2027 (and the skills you need to get them)."
- "What I look for when hiring a freight forwarding manager: attitude over CV."

### Pillar 5: Opinion & Trends
Hot takes on industry news, predictions, contrarian views, lessons from other industries.

**Why it works:** Opinion content drives engagement. It's also the lowest-effort pillar because you're reacting to what's already in the newsfeed.

**Post ideas:**
- "Everyone's talking about nearshoring. Here's why it's not going to replace China sourcing for most companies."
- "The logistics industry has a jargon problem. Stop calling yourself a 'supply chain orchestrator' and tell me what you move."
- "Amazon's logistics strategy isn't about delivery speed anymore — it's about cost. Here's the 2025 playbook."

## Cadence: 3 Posts Per Week

| Day | Pillar | Format | Notes |
|-----|--------|--------|-------|
| **Monday** | Ops & Execution | Short story + takeaway (300–500 words) | Opens the week strong, sets tone |
| **Wednesday** | Tech/Auto or Policy | Insight + actionable tip (200–300 words) | Midweek value, easy to digest |
| **Friday** | Career/Opinion | Lighter take or discussion prompt (100–250 words) | Engages comments over the weekend |

**Why 3×/week?** LinkedIn's algorithm rewards consistency over volume. Two posts per week is the minimum to build any momentum; five is exhausting to maintain. Three hits the sweet spot for a working logistics professional.

## Post Templates

### The Problem–Solution Template
```
[Problem statement — name something specific that happens in logistics]
[1–2 sentences on why it matters operationally or commercially]
[What most people do wrong]
[What actually works — your approach, with one concrete example]
[Call to action — question to the audience]
```

**Example:**
> Most freight forwarders still manage rate negotiations on spreadsheets.
>
> The problem isn't the spreadsheet — it's that by the time you've consolidated carrier responses, the rates have changed.
>
> What I've found works: run mini-bid rounds with your top 5 carriers every 90 days instead of one annual tender. Keeps them honest and your data fresh.
>
> Anyone else moved away from annual RFQs? What did you switch to?

### The "Here's What I Learned" Template
```
[Setting the scene — a project, a mistake, a surprise outcome]
[The conventional wisdom]
[What actually happened]
[What I'd do differently / what I learned]
[Open question]
```

**Example:**
> When I started implementing warehouse automation, I thought the ROI would come from labour reduction.
>
> It didn't. The real savings came from inventory accuracy — fewer mis-picks, less safety stock, fewer chargebacks.
>
> Lesson: don't sell automation to your CFO based on headcount. Sell it based on working capital.
>
> What surprised you most about your first automation project?

### The Hot Take Template
```
[Strong opinion statement]
[Why most people disagree]
[Your reasoning — 2–3 points grounded in operational reality]
[Call to action — agree / disagree?]
```

**Example:**
> 'Supply chain visibility' is the most oversold concept in logistics right now.
>
> I've seen companies spend six figures on visibility platforms and still miss their OTIF targets. Because visibility isn't the problem — execution is.
>
> Knowing where your container is doesn't help if you can't get a chassis to the port on time.
>
> Am I wrong? Tell me where this is working for you.

## Post Checklist

Before publishing every post, ask:

- [ ] **Would a logistics operator care about this?** Not your mum, not a general audience — a freight planner, warehouse manager, or procurement lead.
- [ ] **Is there a concrete example?** If there's no real situation behind the advice, it's generic.
- [ ] **Is the CTA a conversation starter?** "What do you think?" is lazy. Ask something specific to their experience.
- [ ] **Is the language UK English?** Check for -ize spellings creeping in.
- [ ] **Is the first sentence strong enough to stop a scroll?** If it could start 50 different posts, rewrite it.
- [ ] **Does it add one new frame?** The reader should walk away with one lens or insight they didn't have before. If they can't remember anything 30 seconds after reading, the post is forgettable.

## Content Recycling: The 1-to-5 Method

Don't create from scratch. A single piece of analysis or a strong opinion should fuel your content for weeks. This is how you maintain visibility without burning out.

**The Flow:** One core idea becomes five distinct pieces of content.

1.  **The Spark (A Strong Opinion or Analysis):** Start with one core insight. E.g., "The new EU carbon tariffs (CBAM) are going to hit mid-market importers harder than they think."

2.  **Piece 1: The Text-Only Post (LinkedIn):** Your initial "hot take" or observation. Short, punchy, designed to test the idea and spark conversation.
    > *"The EU's CBAM isn't just a compliance headache. For mid-market importers, it's a direct threat to gross margin. Most don't have the data to calculate the embedded carbon in their freight. This is a blind spot. Are you prepared?"*

3.  **Piece 2: The Data Post / Carousel (LinkedIn):** Take the core idea and back it with data. Create a simple chart or a 3-5 slide carousel.
    > *Slide 1: What is CBAM?*
    > *Slide 2: Chart showing the cost impact on a sample £1M import business.*
    > *Slide 3: Three data points you need to start collecting NOW.*
    > *Slide 4: My contact info.*

4.  **Piece 3: The "How-To" Article (LinkedIn Article or Blog):** Expand the idea into a practical, step-by-step guide.
    > *"A 5-Step CBAM Readiness Plan for UK Importers"*

5.  **Piece 4: The Case Study (PDF Download / Featured Section):** If you've helped a client with this, turn it into a one-page PAR case study. This becomes a lead generation asset.
    > *"How We Prepared a Mid-Market 3PL for CBAM and De-risked Their EU Trade Lane"*

6.  **Piece 5: The Video Script (LinkedIn Video):** Record a 2-minute video talking through the core concept. More personal, builds trust.
    > *"Hi everyone, a quick thought on the new EU carbon tariffs..."*

This system means you only need 1-2 core ideas per month to fuel a consistent, high-quality content engine.

## The Zero-Effort Engagement Plan

For days when you are too busy running the business to create original content. This plan takes 15 minutes and keeps your profile active and visible.

**The Goal:** Make five insightful comments on other people's posts.

**The Rules:**
1.  **Follow the Right People:** Your feed should be full of industry leaders, target clients, and relevant publications (use your Audience & Channel Map from the `/personal-brand` skill).
2.  **No "Great post!" comments.** This adds zero value.
3.  **Add to the conversation.** Your comment should be a mini-post in itself.

**Comment Templates:**
-   **The "Agree and Amplify":** *"Great point. I'd add that this is especially true in the [your niche]. For example, we saw [data point or short anecdote]."*
-   **The "Respectful Disagreement":** *"Interesting take. From my experience in [your niche], I've seen the opposite happen. [1-2 sentences explaining your view]. Curious what others think."*
-   **The "Ask a Clarifying Question":** *"This is a critical point. How do you see this applying to [a specific situation, e.g., less-than-container-load shipments]? That's where we often see the process break down."*

Doing this five times a day takes 15 minutes and often generates more profile views and connection requests than posting original content. It positions you as an active expert in the conversation.

## Topics to Avoid

- Generic motivational quotes overlaid on photos (zero signal)
- "The future of supply chain is digital" without naming a single technology
- Reposting news articles without adding operational context
- Self-promotional "I'm so excited to announce" posts (use sparingly — 1 per quarter)
- AI-generated content that sounds like a marketer who's never touched a freight manifest
- Overused phrases: "synergy", "paradigm shift", "ecosystem", "revolutionise", "game-changer"
