# Lead Generation Strategy

A repeatable methodology for identifying, prioritising, and engaging logistics decision-makers. Designed for independent consultants who sell expertise, not software — your leverage is your credibility, so outreach must demonstrate it from the first sentence.

---

## Tiered Account Approach

Not all accounts are worth the same effort. Score each target and invest accordingly.

### Tier 1 — Strategic (high fit, high probability, high value)
**Score threshold:** 80–100  
**Definition:** Existing referral, past client, or company where you have a warm introduction within two hops. Clear budget, clear logistics pain point, decision-maker has a timeline.

**Investment:**
- Custom outreach per contact (45–60 mins prep)
- Follow-up cadence: 3 touches over 14 days
- Offer a diagnostic call format, not a pitch
- Propose a short scoping engagement (paid)

### Tier 2 — Nurture (good fit, needs cultivation)
**Score threshold:** 50–79  
**Definition:** Company fits your ICP — supply chain complexity, mid-to-large revenue, known pain — but no warm intro, no active RFP, or timeline is vague.

**Investment:**
- Semi-personalised outreach using a template (15–20 mins prep)
- Follow-up cadence: 5 touches over 6 weeks
- Share one insight or article per touch (not a generic 'checking in')
- Track for a trigger event (regulatory change, earnings miss, leadership change)

### Tier 3 — Pipeline (long tail, low effort)
**Score threshold:** below 50  
**Definition:** Cold outbound — company fits loose ICP, no referral path, no immediate trigger. Purpose: fill top of funnel for future cultivation.

**Investment:**
- Templated outreach with minor customisation (5 mins)
- Follow-up cadence: 3 touches, then move to nurture list if engaged
- Automate where possible (sequence tools)
- Do not chase beyond three unanswered touches

## Targeting Logistics Decision-Makers

### Role Taxonomy

| Role | Typical Title | Pain Button | Best Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Budget Holder** | VP Supply Chain, Head of Logistics, Director of Operations | Cost-to-serve, missed SLAs, carrier fragmentation | Benchmark data, industry comps, cost-case narrative |
| **Procurement** | Procurement Manager (Logistics), Category Lead | Compliance, framework agreements, standardisation | Structured proposal, rate card review, references |
| **Operations** | Operations Director, Regional Ops Manager | Daily firefighting, driver retention, warehouse throughput | Diagnosis first — ask about their biggest operational headache this quarter |
| **Executive** | CEO, MD (for smaller firms) | Strategic direction, investor narrative, competitive pressure | Big picture — 3-5 year vision, digital maturity, network optimisation |

### Targeting Methodology

1. **Map the account** — Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator / company announcements to list: budget holder, direct reports, procurement contact, CEO/MD.
2. **Identify the entry point** — Procurement is the gate, operations is the referral, budget holder is the decision. Aim for the budget holder, route via operations if you can.
3. **Find the trigger** — Earnings call mention of logistics costs, new warehouse opening, leadership hire, regulatory deadline (e.g. UK Border Target Operating Model, EU CSRD, US FMCSA rules).
4. **Score the contact** — Do they have: decision authority? budget visibility? a stated pain? a timeline? Score 1–3 on each.

## Outreach Templates

### Template A — Warm Intro (Tier 1)

**Subject:** [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out — [specific trigger]

Hi [First name],

[Mutual connection] mentioned you're [specific context — e.g. restructuring your European distribution network]. That's exactly the kind of problem I help logistics leadership teams work through.

I spent [X years] at [relevant company/background] doing [relevant thing], and I now work independently with companies like [client example — anonymised if needed] on [problem area].

Would you be open to 20 minutes to compare notes on [trigger topic]? No agenda beyond that — I'm genuinely curious what you're seeing.

[Your name]

---

### Template B — Trigger-Based Outreach (Tier 1–2)

**Subject:** [Trigger] — what I'm seeing across the sector

Hi [First name],

I saw [specific trigger — e.g. the announcement about your new DC in Daventry]. It caught my attention because I've been working with a few operators facing the same challenge: [specific pain linked to trigger].

A couple of patterns I'm noticing:

- [Insight 1 — specific, data-backed]
- [Insight 2 — specific, data-backed]

If any of this resonates, I'd be happy to share a few notes. If not, no problem at all.

Best,

[Your name]

---

### Template C — Cold Outreach (Tier 2–3)

**Subject:** Quick question about [specific observation]

Hi [First name],

I've been looking at [company name]'s network setup — specifically [one specific, non-obvious observation — e.g. the mix of owned and hired fleet across your Midlands operation].

Most operators I speak to are dealing with [common problem relevant to your observation]. The ones making the fastest progress are doing [one thing differently].

My work is helping logistics teams bridge that gap without the usual long consulting cycles.

If that's a conversation worth having, let me know.

[Your name]

## Follow-Up Cadence (Tier 1)

| Touch | Day | Medium | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | Email | Initial outreach (Template A or B) |
| 2 | 3–4 | LinkedIn | Like/comment on their post; send connection request if not connected |
| 3 | 7 | Email | Short follow-up — one additional insight or article, no 'just checking in' |
| 4 | 10 | Phone/Loom | Brief voice note or video: "Thought this might land better spoken than typed" |
| 5 | 14 | Email | Break-up email — "Assuming timing isn't right, no worries. If things change I'm here." |

For Tier 2, spread the same touches over 6 weeks. For Tier 3, stop after 3 touches if no response.

## Networking Intelligence

Logistics is a relationship business. Build your intelligence systematically.

- **Conference list:** Maintain a calendar of Freight Week (UK), TPM (US), Transport Logistic (EU), LogiSYM (APAC). Note which contacts are speaking / attending.
- **Press monitoring:** Set up Google Alerts for top 20 target accounts.
- **Referral loops:** Every intro call ends with: *"Who else in your network should I be talking to?"* Track these in your pipeline.
- **Advisor roles:** One advisory board seat or non-exec role per year confers credibility and access across the sector.

## 90-Day Go-to-Market Plan Template

| Phase | Weeks | Activity | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Set-up** | 1–2 | Define ICP, score top 20 accounts, prepare outreach materials | Target list, templates, tracking sheet |
| **Active outreach** | 3–6 | 5–10 personalised outreaches per week (Tier 1 focus); follow up Tier 2 | Discovery calls booked |
| **Conversion** | 7–10 | 10–15 discovery calls; diagnostic proposals to qualified leads | Scoping engagements / proposals submitted |
| **Review & iterate** | 11–12 | Win-loss analysis on all outcomes; refresh target list; refine messaging | Updated targets, improved templates, pipeline review |
