# Win-Loss Analysis Loop

A systematic approach to capturing, analysing, and acting on every sales outcome. The goal is not to justify a loss, but to extract intelligence that compounds your competitive advantage over time.

## Debrief Process (for Lost Deals)

1.  **Gather Facts (30 mins):** Complete the capture template honestly. No sugar-coating.
2.  **Request Feedback (5 mins):** Send a simple, professional request to the client.
    > *"Thanks again for the opportunity. To help us improve, would you be open to sharing the primary factor that drove your decision? Any feedback on our process would be a huge help."*
3.  **Identify the Gap (15 mins):** Classify the loss using the 3-Gap framework below. Be specific.
4.  **Define the Action (10 mins):** What is the single, concrete change you will make in your next sales cycle? Write it down.

## 3-Gap Analysis

Every loss can be traced to one of these three gaps.

### Gap 1 — Knowledge Gap
*"We didn't know enough about their problem, politics, or process."*
- **Signs:** You were surprised by a stakeholder, a budget constraint, or a technical requirement late in the process.
- **Remedies:** Deepen pre-call research. Use the first call *only* for discovery. Ask explicitly: "Who else is involved in this decision?"

### Gap 2 — Alignment Gap
*"We had the right expertise, but failed to connect it to their specific pain point."*
- **Signs:** The client said your proposal was "technically impressive" but chose a "simpler" option. They couldn't see how your solution would work in their reality.
- **Remedies:** Use their exact language in your proposal. Include a "Day in the Life After" section. Offer a small, paid diagnostic phase to prove the fit.

### Gap 3 — Execution Gap
*"Our sales process itself was the weakness."*
- **Signs:** You were slow to respond, the proposal had errors, you didn't have a clear champion, or the competitor was simply more professional.
- **Remedies:** Institute a 24-hour response rule. Create a pre-flight checklist for all proposals. Explicitly ask your champion: "What will it take for us to win this?"

## Pattern Synthesis (Quarterly Review)

This is the most critical loop. It turns raw data into a refined strategy.

### Step 1 — Aggregate Outcomes
- **Total Opportunities Decided:**
- **Win Rate %:** (Won / Total)
- **Average Deal Value (Won vs. Lost):**
- **Tally of Primary Gaps:** (Knowledge / Alignment / Execution)

### Step 2 — Synthesise and Action
Review the patterns and write down specific actions that feed back into your other processes. This is not an academic exercise.

- **Pattern:** Losing on price consistently to larger firms.
    - **Analysis:** Alignment Gap. We aren't justifying the premium.
    - **Action:** Refine personal brand. Use the `/personal-brand` skill to update your positioning statement to focus on a niche where you are the undisputed expert (e.g., "The go-to consultant for post-merger logistics integration in the UK pharma sector").

- **Pattern:** Pitches are technically strong but prospects call them "too complex".
    - **Analysis:** Alignment Gap. We are selling the 'how', not the 'what'.
    - **Action:** Refine pitch. Use the `/pitch-master` skill to run mock sessions focused on simplifying the core message and leading with the outcome, not the methodology.

- **Pattern:** Getting surprised by procurement stakeholders late in the deal.
    - **Analysis:** Knowledge Gap. Our initial discovery is weak.
    - **Action:** Refine lead generation. Update the `references/lead-gen-strategy.md` to include a mandatory question in the first call: "Who else, besides yourself, will be involved in evaluating this proposal, including from procurement or finance?"

- **Pattern:** Losing deals we should be winning because of slow follow-up.
    - **Analysis:** Execution Gap. Our process is sloppy.
    - **Action:** Refine pipeline management. Enforce the "Next Action" rule in `references/pipeline-tracker.md` with zero tolerance. If a 'next action' is more than 2 days overdue, it's a critical failure.

- **Pattern:** Winning most deals that come from referrals, but losing most cold outreach.
    - **Analysis:** We have a trust deficit in cold outreach.
    - **Action:** Refine lead generation strategy. Double down on referral generation. For cold outreach, pivot from "here's what I do" to offering a specific, high-value insight in the first email, using templates from `references/lead-gen-strategy.md`.

### Step 3 — Define Quarterly Test
Based on the synthesis, define one thing you will test for the next 90 days.
- **Example:** "For this quarter, every proposal will be preceded by a one-page 'Understanding Document' sent to the prospect to confirm we have correctly captured their problem, before we write the full SOW."

---

## Closing Note

Winning is a lagging indicator. The leading indicator of success is the speed at which you learn from your market. A rigorously documented loss is more valuable than an unexamined win. It is the raw material for your future advantage.
